Early Life

Marie Curie was born Maria Sk≈Çodowska on November 7, 1867, in Warsaw, Poland.[1]

Education

In 1891, Curie moved to Paris to attend the Sorbonne University, where she earned degrees in physics and mathematics.[2]

Scientific Work

Curie's pioneering research on radioactivity, which she coined, included the discovery of the elements polonium and radium.[3]

Nobel Prizes

Marie Curie was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1903, along with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel, for their work on radioactivity. In 1911, she received a second Nobel Prize in Chemistry for her discovery of radium and polonium.[4]

Later Life

Marie Curie continued her scientific work until her death on July 4, 1934, from aplastic anemia, believed to be caused by her prolonged exposure to radiation.[5]

Legacy

Marie Curie's legacy lives on through the numerous awards and honors named after her, as well as her lasting impact on the fields of physics and chemistry.[6]
